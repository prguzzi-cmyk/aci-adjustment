Water damage done to your property may or may not be covered by an insurance policy. It mainly depends on the cause of the damage and the terms of the policy.

As a general principle, the damage caused by something inevitable within the property is covered and that due to lack of maintenance or neglect is not.

However, it is not always easy to draw a line between what's covered and what's not.
<br/>
<br/>

## When Does Homeowners Insurance Cover Water Damage From Toilet Overflow?

In most of the standard home insurance policies, the water damage that is sudden and internal is covered.

It means that if sudden and accidental tearing, cracking, burning, or bulging of a steam, heating, AC, or automatic fire protection system are the causes of damage, it will be covered.

Damages caused by an overflowing toilet are only covered if the overflow is due to a sump pump, drain blockage, or any other cause from within the building.
<br/>
<br/>

## Does Homeowners Insurance Cover Water Damage From Toilet Overflow Always?

If a toilet overflow is caused by any of the following, the resulting damages are generally not covered by insurance.

- Ground seepage
- Water or sewer pipe backups
- Flooding
- Poorly-maintained pipes leading to leaks
  <br/>
  <br/>

## Mold Caused by Water Damage - The Costly Part

Toilet overflow is not that much of a problem in itself. It leads to the propagation of mold on the floor and wooden surfaces.

Mold is not always covered by standard insurance policies. It can cost upwards of $30,000 to get mold removed from your property.

However, there are some cases where mold is covered by insurance. Let's see what those cases are.
<br/>
<br/>

### When is the Damage Caused By Mold Covered by Insurance?

As a general principle any damage resulting from negligence or lack of appropriate action is not covered by insurance. The cases when mold is covered by insurance policy include:

- If the water seepage is hidden and the homeowner could not possibly see it.
- If a mold is caused by flooding, flood insurance will cover it.
- Insurance agencies in the states with higher humidity like Florida and Louisiana cover mold damage.
